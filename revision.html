HTML introduction

HTML stands for hypertext markup language

Made up of tags, attributes and content

  • Tags are enclosed in "< >".
  • Attributes provide additional information to HTML elements, they are placed in the opening tag and modify an elements. They consist of a name and value, separated by an = sign, enclosed in "".
  • Content is the text displayed.

Tags 2 know

  • title - this adds text to the top of a tab
  • h1...h6 - headings, 1 is the biggest, then 2 etc.
  • p - paragraph
  • em - emphasis, italics
  • strong - strong emphasis, bold
  • ul/ol - lists, indent each item with li
  • a - anchors items
  • header, article, section, footer - parts of a document

Markup

Two parts of a document are:

  1. Head - this defines meta information about the HTML document, includes title, stylesheets etc
  2. Body - main content of the webpage

Markup is either procedural or descriptive

Procedural markup is how the web page looks

Descriptive markup is what the information means

3 types of descriptive:

  • Stratified - separates content from presentation
  • Dynamic - different presentation to suit circumstances
  • Semantic - enables machine processing

Overall

We use descriptive markup that has semantic value, improving information quality and styling of pages must be achieved outside of HTML
